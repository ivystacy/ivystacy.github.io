Escalator Project (Machine Learning)

Python | Jupyter Notebook

During my summer internship at the Port of Seattle, my team and I worked on implementing and testing preliminary machine learning models to detect dangerous objects (such as suitcases, strollers, ect.) on airport escalators, aiming to improve the safety of travellers.

Over six weeks, the machine learning team worked on various methods of detection. Using transfer learning, I worked on a binary classification model that would sort images displaying different escalator situations into two categories, safe and unsafe. Even though our dataset was limited, the model was able to sort the images at an accuracy rate of 81%.

The preparation, training and testing process can be viewed in depth here.

Bubble Tea Generator

JavaScript | App Lab

This was one of my favorite projects that I worked on while participating in CSE Startup at the University of Washington. We were to create a user-centered app that was simple to understand and easy to navigate. I decided to implement an app targeted to indecisive bubble tea customers where they are able to generate random drinks and then save their favorite combinations.

In the making of this project, various trials were completed to determine whether users were able to complete a series of tasks using the app. With this, we frequently took user feedback and made adjustments to ensure that the app was as user-friendly as possible. Check out the final app here.

Ctrl Save

HTML | CSS | JavaScript

I was first introduced to computer science when I participated in the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program. Over the summer, I gained exposure to Python, HTML, Arduino and much more. As the final project, I collaborated on an interactive website 'Ctrl Save' which aimed raise awareness for endangered species.

When you enter the 'Ctrl Save' webpage, you first play a game that intends to capture the POV of an endangered animal. The moment you lose, you are redirected to a page where there is information about the actions you can take to help at-risk species. This was the first coding project that I ever worked on, so there are some imperfections, but it provided a great learning experience for my classmates and I!
